# Stonegate Lead Manager User Manual

Last updated: July 23, 2026

## Who This Guide Is For

This guide is for the person responsible for responding to warm seller leads, learning what the
seller needs, deciding the correct next step, and scheduling qualified appointments.

You do not need technical knowledge to use Stonegate. The instructions below use the same names
that appear on the screen.

## Your Job In One Sentence

Respond quickly, understand the seller's situation, and make sure every active seller has a clear
owner and a dated next action.

## Your Main Responsibilities

- Accept new warm leads quickly.
- Contact the seller using an allowed communication method.
- Complete the seller qualification questions.
- Decide whether the seller needs an appointment, follow-up, nurture, or disqualification.
- Schedule qualified appointments with an available closer.
- Keep notes, tasks, and seller information current.
- Make sure no active seller is forgotten.
- Give the closer a complete and accurate handoff.

## What You Should Not Do

- Do not promise a price or guarantee that Stonegate will buy the property.
- Do not change an approved offer amount.
- Do not send a contract unless your role specifically allows it.
- Do not contact someone who has opted out or is marked Do Not Contact.
- Do not invent answers when the seller does not know something.
- Do not create a duplicate lead because information is missing.
- Do not mark a task complete before the work is actually finished.
- Do not treat a Copilot suggestion as a confirmed fact.

## The Most Important Rule

Before leaving an active seller record, confirm all three items:

1. The correct person owns the lead.
2. The next action is clear.
3. The next action has a future date and time.

If one of these is missing, the lead can be forgotten.

## Starting Your Day

Follow this order every workday:

1. Open **Lead Desk**.
2. Look at the numbers across the top.
3. Open **Daily queue**.
4. Accept every new warm handoff assigned to you.
5. Work any overdue seller follow-ups.
6. Review today's appointments.
7. Resolve neglected leads.
8. Open **Inbox > Needs reply**.
9. Open **Work Queue > My work**.
10. Check **Calendar** before offering appointment times.

Work new warm leads before routine follow-up. Fast response matters.

## Understanding The Lead Desk

The numbers at the top tell you what needs attention:

- **Awaiting acceptance:** New warm leads waiting for someone to take responsibility.
- **Overdue SLA:** Warm leads that were not accepted within the required response time.
- **Qualification due:** Sellers who still need the qualification questions completed.
- **Follow-ups due:** Promised calls, texts, or emails that are due.
- **Appointments today:** Seller appointments scheduled for today.
- **Neglected:** Active sellers without proper follow-up protection.

Red or warning-styled numbers should be handled first.

The Lead Desk contains these tabs:

- **Copilot**
- **Daily queue**
- **Qualification**
- **Performance**
- **Standards**, for authorized managers only

## Accepting A New Warm Lead

When a VA, public form, campaign, or team member creates a warm handoff:

1. Open **Lead Desk**.
2. Select **Daily queue**.
3. Find **Accept warm handoffs**.
4. Review the seller name, property, source, assigned person, and acceptance deadline.
5. Click **Accept**.
6. Use the open-record icon to review the full lead when needed.
7. Open the seller in **Inbox** and read the conversation history before contacting them.

Clicking **Accept** means you are taking responsibility for the lead. It does not mean the seller
is qualified or that Stonegate has agreed to buy the property.

## Contacting The Seller

Open **Inbox** and select the seller conversation.

Before contacting the seller:

1. Read the entire recent conversation.
2. Confirm the seller's preferred contact method.
3. Check SMS consent and Do Not Contact status.
4. Check the seller's local time.
5. Review the assigned owner and next task.

The middle of the Inbox contains the full timeline. It can include:

- Text messages
- Emails
- Calls
- Call recordings
- Transcripts
- Internal notes
- Delivery or provider updates

Use the correct composer:

- **SMS:** Send a text when SMS is configured and allowed.
- **Email:** Send an email when the mailbox is connected.
- **Call:** Start or record a call.
- **Note:** Add internal information that the seller must not receive.

Never place private staff comments in an SMS or email message.

If a communication service is not connected, contact the seller through the approved company
method and log the result in Stonegate.

## A Simple Seller Conversation

Use the approved questions on the screen, but speak naturally.

1. **Open:** Introduce yourself, identify Stonegate, and confirm it is a good time to talk.
2. **Reason:** Ask what has the seller considering a sale now.
3. **Property:** Confirm occupancy, condition, repairs, and access.
4. **People:** Confirm ownership and everyone who must agree to a sale.
5. **Timing:** Ask when the seller would ideally like the sale completed.
6. **Price:** Ask whether the seller has a price in mind without promising Stonegate's offer.
7. **Next step:** Agree on a specific call, message, or appointment.
8. **Confirm:** Repeat the next step and make sure the seller has no unanswered question.

Listen more than you speak. Your goal is to understand the seller and arrange the right next step,
not to pressure the seller or debate the property's value.

### Useful notes

A useful note is specific:

> Seller lives out of state. Property is vacant. Seller reports roof age is unknown and the
> kitchen needs updating. Sister is also on title and must approve. Requested a call Thursday at
> 2:00 PM after speaking with her.

An unhelpful note is vague:

> Good lead. Follow up later.

## Completing Seller Qualification

Complete qualification during or immediately after the seller conversation.

1. Open **Lead Desk**.
2. Select **Qualification**.
3. Choose the seller from **Needs qualification**.
4. Ask the displayed questions naturally.
5. Enter the seller's answers accurately.
6. Select the correct **Next action**.
7. Choose a future **Due date and time** when required.
8. Click **Complete qualification**.

The standard questions cover:

- Who owns the property and how title is held?
- Who must agree to sell?
- Why is the seller considering a sale?
- When does the seller want the sale completed?
- What repairs or updates are needed?
- Who currently occupies the property?
- Does the seller have a price in mind?
- Is there a mortgage, lien, or other balance?
- How and when can the property be viewed?

Use the seller's words when possible. If the seller does not know, enter that they do not know. Do
not guess.

## When A Seller Is Ready For An Appointment

A seller is generally ready for an appointment when:

- You have confirmed the correct property and seller.
- The owner and important decision-makers are known.
- The seller has a real reason and timeframe for considering a sale.
- You understand the basic condition and occupancy.
- The seller is willing to let Stonegate evaluate the property or discuss the sale.
- The appointment has a clear purpose.

Missing optional details do not always prevent an appointment. Record what is unknown and make it
part of the closer's preparation. Escalate unusual ownership, title, legal, safety, or compliance
issues instead of trying to solve them yourself.

## Choosing The Correct Next Action

| Next action | Use it when |
| --- | --- |
| **Call** | You promised another phone call or need missing information |
| **Text** | The seller asked for a text or a short follow-up is appropriate |
| **Email** | The seller prefers email or needs written information |
| **Seller appointment** | The seller is qualified and ready to meet |
| **Nurture follow-up** | The seller may become ready later but is not ready now |
| **Disqualify** | The lead is not a fit and no continued follow-up is appropriate |

All choices except **Disqualify** require a future date and time.

Choose the next action that reflects what will actually happen. Do not select **Seller
appointment** merely to improve a scorecard.

## Scheduling A Seller Appointment

Selecting **Seller appointment** during qualification places the seller in the appointment
scheduling queue. The appointment is not fully dispatched until a closer and time are confirmed.

To schedule it:

1. Open **Field Operations**.
2. Select **Dispatch**.
3. Choose the seller under **Needs appointment**.
4. Enter the requested start and end times.
5. Click **Check capacity**.
6. Review the available closers.
7. Select an eligible closer.
8. Choose the appointment type:
   - Seller appointment
   - Property walkthrough
   - Offer presentation
9. Choose the location:
   - Property
   - Office
   - Phone
   - Video
10. Add a preparation note with access instructions and seller expectations.
11. Click **Dispatch appointment**.
12. Open **Calendar** and confirm the appointment appears correctly.

Do not override a closer conflict unless you have manager authority and a legitimate reason.

## What Makes A Good Handoff To The Closer

Before the appointment, confirm the record contains:

- Seller's main reason for selling.
- Desired timeline.
- Decision-makers.
- Occupancy.
- Property condition and known repair concerns.
- Asking-price expectation, if provided.
- Mortgage or lien information, if known.
- Access instructions.
- The seller's preferred communication method.
- Important objections, promises, or concerns.
- Correct appointment date, time, location, and closer.

The closer should not need to call you for basic information that the seller already provided.

## Using The Lead Manager Copilot

The Copilot helps organize information. It does not replace your judgment.

To use it:

1. Open **Lead Desk > Copilot**.
2. Select a seller under **Needs attention**.
3. Review the system's recommended action and alerts.
4. Click **Generate brief**.
5. Compare the brief with the seller record and conversation.
6. Review:
   - Summary
   - Why the seller is prioritized
   - Handoff information
   - Missing qualification details
   - Suggested questions
   - Draft message
   - Proposed task
   - Appointment suggestion
   - Evidence and risks
7. Choose one review result:
   - **Accept brief:** It is accurate as written.
   - **Correct:** Fix the summary or message, explain the correction, and save it.
   - **Reject:** It is not useful or contains important errors.

The Copilot cannot:

- Send the draft message.
- Schedule an appointment.
- Change seller facts.
- Transfer ownership.
- Promise or approve an offer.

After reviewing the Copilot, perform the real action yourself in Inbox, Work Queue, Qualification,
or Field Operations.

## Working The Inbox

Use these Inbox views:

- **Mine:** Conversations assigned to you.
- **Unassigned:** Conversations that need an owner.
- **Team:** Team conversations you are allowed to see.
- **Needs reply:** Sellers waiting for a response.
- **Appointments:** Sellers with appointment activity.
- **Unread:** Conversations you have not reviewed.

For each conversation:

1. Read the newest message and enough history to understand it.
2. Confirm consent and contact eligibility.
3. Respond or log the completed contact.
4. Add an internal note when staff needs context.
5. Update assignment when responsibility changes.
6. Create a dated follow-up before leaving the conversation.

## Working The Work Queue

Open **Work Queue** and use:

- **My work:** Tasks assigned to you.
- **Overdue:** Tasks already late.
- **Due next:** Tasks that need attention soon.
- **Unscheduled:** Tasks missing a proper date.
- **All work:** Team work you are allowed to see.

Start with **Overdue**, then work **Due next**.

Communication tasks open the conversation. Appointment tasks open the calendar. Other tasks open
the full lead.

Mark a task complete only after the call, text, email, review, or follow-up is actually finished.

## Using All Leads And Seller Pipeline

Use **All Leads** when you need to find a seller.

You can search by:

- Seller name
- Property address
- Phone
- Email
- Lead source

Use **Seller Pipeline** to see where active sellers are in the process. Move a seller to a new
stage only when that real event has happened.

Do not create another lead simply because you cannot find the record immediately. Search by the
property address and phone number first.

## When To Nurture A Lead

Choose **Nurture follow-up** when the seller may be a fit but is not ready now.

Examples:

- The seller wants to wait until after a family event.
- A tenant must move first.
- Probate or title work is still in progress.
- The seller is collecting information before deciding.
- The timeline is several months away.

Set a realistic follow-up date based on the seller's situation. Record why that date was chosen.

Nurture is not a place to hide leads. Every nurture lead still needs a meaningful next action.

## When To Disqualify A Lead

Disqualify only when continued follow-up is not appropriate.

Examples may include:

- The person has no authority or connection to the property.
- The property or situation is outside Stonegate's approved business rules.
- The seller clearly requests no further contact.
- The record is confirmed to be invalid.
- Management has approved closing the opportunity for a documented reason.

Record the reason clearly. If the seller opted out, also confirm the proper suppression or Do Not
Contact status.

## Handling Common Situations

### The seller does not answer

1. Log the call attempt.
2. Use an allowed follow-up channel.
3. Set the next attempt date and time.
4. Do not repeatedly contact the seller outside approved policy.

### The seller asks for a price

Explain that Stonegate must review the property and situation before confirming an offer. Do not
guess or promise a number.

### The seller changes the appointment

Update the existing appointment. Do not create a second appointment unless the original record
cannot be rescheduled.

### The seller asks to stop messages

Stop the outreach. Record the request and confirm the contact is suppressed. Do not send another
promotional message.

### The property information conflicts

Record what the seller said and flag the conflict. Do not overwrite confirmed evidence with an
assumption.

### The Copilot is wrong

Choose **Correct** or **Reject**. Add a short explanation so the system can be evaluated and
improved.

### A lead is missing from your queue

Search **All Leads** by phone and property address. Check the assigned owner, current stage, and
whether the lead is archived. Ask a manager to correct access or assignment if needed.

### A page says access is unavailable

Confirm you are signed in with your own Stonegate account. Sign out and sign back in once. If the
problem remains, ask an Owner or Administrator to verify your active role and permissions.

## Your Performance Scorecard

The **Performance** tab shows the trailing 30 days:

- Warm handoffs received and accepted.
- Handoffs accepted within the required time.
- Average acceptance time.
- Qualifications completed.
- Appointments set.
- Appointments held.
- No-shows.
- Contracts created from your leads.
- Follow-up quality.

The goal is not to create the largest number of appointments. The goal is to respond quickly,
qualify accurately, protect follow-up, and schedule appointments that have a reasonable chance of
moving forward.

## End-Of-Day Checklist

Before signing out:

- No warm handoff assigned to you is waiting for acceptance.
- No seller in **Needs reply** is waiting without a reason.
- No overdue task assigned to you is ignored.
- Every active lead has a dated next action.
- Tomorrow's appointments are confirmed.
- Appointment notes and access instructions are complete.
- Important calls and messages are logged.
- Copilot drafts you used were accepted, corrected, or rejected.
- Any blocked lead has been escalated to the correct manager.

## Getting Help

Contact an Owner or Administrator when:

- Your navigation is missing a page required for your job.
- A lead belongs to the wrong person and you cannot reassign it.
- A duplicate record must be corrected.
- Consent or Do Not Contact information is unclear.
- A communication provider repeatedly fails.
- An appointment requires a conflict override.
- The seller situation may require legal, compliance, or management review.
- You are unsure whether a lead should be disqualified.

When asking for help, provide the seller name, property address, what you were trying to do, and
the exact message shown on the screen. Never send passwords or API credentials.
